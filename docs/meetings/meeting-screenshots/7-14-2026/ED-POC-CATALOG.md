## 4. Gaps / Risks — what the direction under-weights or omits

Honest assessment: DIRECTION-v2 is a strong rebase and covers the *big* mechanics (dashboards+reports, chat-builds-charts, save-to-dashboard, drill-down, provenance, lenses, export-as-actions, ephemeral/present). But several concrete things Ed **showed and emphasized** are dropped or thinned. Ranked by risk.

**1. The Insights surface got quietly downsized (highest interpretive risk).**
Ed's Insights is his stated "wow factor" and the thing he "landed on first" (16:02, 22:05, 27:26). In the POC it is a **rich, full three-column workspace**: an AI "TODAY" narrative, a scrollable list of finding-cards, a "surprising links" correlation row, and a **focus panel** where clicking a card swaps in a chart + a critiquing **BRICKLAYER'S READ** + **DIG DEEPER follow-up chips** + an inline **Ask** box. DIRECTION-v2 §6 reduces this to "a light Home/Briefing = the day's 2–3 findings" + pinned inline cards. That is defensible against Cody's duplication concern (27:03) — but it risks **throwing out the interaction Ed is most proud of**. Recommend explicitly designing the Insights **focus-panel + follow-up-chips + AI-read** pattern rather than collapsing Insights to a light landing. This is the single biggest divergence between the POC and the written direction.

**2. No MAP in the widget library (concrete omission).**
The **Property distribution map** is a prominent, recurring tile (screenshots 11, 21) and geographic exposure/over-exposure is a use case Ed named explicitly (17:08, transcript "where they are landed, where they're overexposed"). The §4 library (9 HubSpot types + scatter/pivot/combination/list) **has no map**. This should be added as a first-class widget type. High priority, low ambiguity.

**3. No heatmap / correlation-matrix type.**
Two heatmap variants render prominently — the **valuation-staleness heatmap** (class × age, green→red) and the **"what moves with what" correlation matrix** — plus a **"Trend integrity (Simpson's-paradox check)"** widget in the library modal. None are in §4's list. Add a heatmap/matrix primitive.

**4. The "Explain this" right-click interaction is under-specified.**
Ed spent real time on it (25:29–26:22): **right-click a risk-watchlist row → "Explain this property" → agent reasons in chat → "Open property workspace."** DIRECTION-v2 folds "explanation" into generic co-working chat, but loses (a) the **right-click context menu on a tile/table row** as an entry point and (b) the **watchlist row → chat → property workspace** hand-off. Worth calling out as a named pattern; it's one of Ed's clearest "this is what we want people to feel" moments.

**5. No interactive scenario / stress-test widget.**
The Credit-Officer board's **Direct-cap reprice** tile has **editable Cap/Occ inputs** driving scenario rows (screenshot 23) — a genuine what-if/input widget on a dashboard. Nothing in §4 covers an interactive/parameterized widget. This blurs the §3 A/B "input-vs-output" split (it's an output widget that also takes input in-place) and should be an explicit widget class.

**6. The individual-property workspace is named but not specced.**
Ed tied this to his "very HubSpot / do things on your own" point (30:35–30:58). The POC property view is dense and domain-specific: **valuation-approaches variance bar, cap-rate sensitivity grid, comparable-sales table with CoV badge, appraisal-detail table (appraiser, license, flood zone, zoning, parking), appraisal-signals, and "Worth a check" anomaly flags.** DIRECTION-v2 lists "Properties" as an altitude but enumerates none of these. When the widget library is drawn (Artifact priority #1), the **property-view widget set** needs the same treatment as the portfolio widgets, or it will be under-built.

**7. Terminology friction — "board" is everywhere in the client's build.**
DIRECTION-v2 §5 kills the word "Boards" in favor of "Dashboards." Fair for model-alignment, but note the **POC uses "board" pervasively and load-bearingly**: "Open the full board," "Pin to board," "Full board" tab, "New board," "Build this board," "NEW BOARD READY." The rename is real change the client will feel in every surface; worth flagging for the Cody ratification (§9) so it's a conscious decision, not a silent one.

**8. Chat is docked LEFT in the POC and it drives board-building — in tension with §2's "right only" rule.**
Throughout the POC the **ASK BRICKLAYER rail is on the LEFT**, and it is the surface that returns charts *and* **builds entire dashboards on the fly** (the "most powerful function"). DIRECTION-v2 §2 argues chat docks **right** because it's "an assistant over existing dashboards, not the driving force." But by Direction's own stated rule (*left = the driving force, Lovable/v0/Cursor-style build-everything*), Ed's board-building chat **qualifies as left-docked**. There's also no separate "full-page threaded chat" in the POC — one left rail does everything inline. This isn't necessarily wrong in the direction, but the **left-vs-right conclusion contradicts the client's own POC**, and the two-surface split is a Direction invention not grounded in what Ed showed. Flag for Cody: reconcile deliberately rather than assume the POC will move to a right dock.

**9. Minor, but real:** the **"BRICKLAYER'S READ" that critiques its own data** (screenshot 7 — "almost certainly a data-entry artifact… rerun the regression") is a distinctive trust/credibility behavior that maps perfectly to the "analyst with receipts" positioning (§5/§8) yet isn't mentioned; the **global FROM/TO date filter** and **"Worth a check" anomaly badges** are small affordances not carried into the direction.

**Bottom line:** The direction is not a rubber-stamp-clean match. The mechanics are largely covered, but three concrete widget types Ed rendered are **missing from the library** (map, heatmap/matrix, interactive scenario), the **Insights experience is materially downsized** from what Ed showcased, the **"Explain this" right-click** and **property-view widget set** are under-specified, and there's an unresolved **left-vs-right chat contradiction** with the POC. All are addressable before the redraw — recommend folding items #1–#6 into Artifact priority #1 (Widget Library) and #2 (IA), and raising #7–#8 in the §9 Cody ratification.
